CMSC 150 - Greenvale Mitigation Planner

R and RStudio

The Greenvale Mitigation Planner is a decision support tool designed for the fictional City of Greenvale. Its primary purpose is to assist the city council in creating an optimal environmental action plan to meet strict national government pollution mandates. The app analyzes 30 different mitigation projects (ranging from renewable energy to public transport upgrades), each with specific costs and pollutant reduction capabilities. It mathematically calculates the exact combination of these projects needed to satisfy 10 different pollution reduction targets simultaneously. The tool utilizes the Dual Simplex Linear Programming (LP) algorithm. This mathematical method allows the system to sort through infinite combinations of fractional project units to find the global minimum cost‚Äîensuring the city spends the least amount of money possible while strictly meeting every environmental requirement.
